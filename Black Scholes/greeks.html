Delta: Delta typically measures the responsiveness of the option price with a $1 change in the underlying price. One can think of delta in 3 ways.

  1. How many futures to buy/sell in order to hedge against 100 of the same option
  2. The probability that the option will expire in the money
  3. How responsive the option price is with regard to a change in the underlying price.

Calls: δ[0,1]
Puts: δ[-1,0]

Description: Take for example a call option with δ = 0.5, its price will increase by $0.50 with every $1 increase in the underlying price.
On the other hand, a put option with δ = -0.4 signifies that its price will increase by $0.40 with every $1 decrease in the underlying price.





Gamma: Gamma typically measures the rate of change of an option's delta for a $1 move in the underlying asset's price. If an option has high gamma,it means that its delta will change drastically with a small change in price, potentially leading to it being more sensitive to price changes.

Gamma is highest when the option is at-the-money, and lowest for options which are deep in- or out-the-money.
